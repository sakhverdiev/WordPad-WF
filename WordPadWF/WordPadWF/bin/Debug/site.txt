</div>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Raymond_III,_Count_of_Tripoli" title="Raymond III, Count of Tripoli">Raymond III</a></b> (1140â€“1187) was the <a href="/wiki/Count_of_Tripoli" title="Count of Tripoli">count of Tripoli</a> (in modern-day Lebanon) from 1152 until his death. The son of <a href="/wiki/Raymond_II,_Count_of_Tripoli" title="Raymond II, Count of Tripoli">Raymond&#160;II</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hodierna_of_Jerusalem" title="Hodierna of Jerusalem">Hodierna of Jerusalem</a>, he was a <a href="/wiki/Minor_(law)" title="Minor (law)">minor</a> when he succeeded his father. After reaching the age of majority in 1155, he fought the powerful Muslim ruler <a href="/wiki/Nur_ad-Din_(died_1174)" title="Nur ad-Din (died 1174)">Nur ad-Din</a>, and hired pirates to pillage <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine</a> territories. From 1164, he spent nearly ten years in Muslim captivity. Marrying the wealthy heiress <a href="/wiki/Eschiva_of_Bures" title="Eschiva of Bures">Eschiva of Bures</a> made him <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Galilee" title="Principality of Galilee">prince of Galilee</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Jerusalem" title="Kingdom of Jerusalem">Kingdom of Jerusalem</a>. A close relative of the royal family, he ruled Jerusalem as <a href="/wiki/Regent" title="Regent">regent</a> for <a href="/wiki/Baldwin_IV_of_Jerusalem" title="Baldwin IV of Jerusalem">Baldwin IV</a> between 1174 and 1176, and for the child <a href="/wiki/Baldwin_V_of_Jerusalem" title="Baldwin V of Jerusalem">Baldwin&#160;V</a> from 1185 to 1186. After the child king's death, he could not prevent the coronation of Baldwin&#160;V's mother <a href="/wiki/Sibylla,_Queen_of_Jerusalem" title="Sibylla, Queen of Jerusalem">Sibylla</a> and her husband <a href="/wiki/Guy_of_Lusignan" title="Guy of Lusignan">Guy of Lusignan</a>. He paid homage to Guy only after Jerusalem was invaded by <a href="/wiki/Saladin" title="Saladin">Saladin</a>, the Muslim ruler of Egypt and Syria. One of the few crusader leaders who escaped from the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hattin" title="Battle of Hattin">battlefield at Hattin</a>, he died soon after, probably of <a href="/wiki/Pleurisy" title="Pleurisy">pleurisy</a>. (<b><a href="/wiki/Raymond_III,_Count_of_Tripoli" title="Raymond III, Count of Tripoli">Full&#160;article...</a></b>)

<p><a href="/wiki/Harry_Kane" title="Harry Kane">Harry Kane</a> is an English professional footballer who represents the <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England national team</a> as a forward. Since making his debut in 2015, he has <b><a href="/wiki/List_of_international_goals_scored_by_Harry_Kane" title="List of international goals scored by Harry Kane">scored 59 goals</a></b> in 86 <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">appearances</a>, becoming his country's all-time record goalscorer. He scored his first goal in his debut in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying">UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Lithuania_national_football_team" title="Lithuania national football team">Lithuania</a> at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a>. Fifty-two of his 59 goals have come in competitive fixtures, with 12 of them occurring at <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cups</a> and <a href="/wiki/UEFA_European_Championship" title="UEFA European Championship">UEFA European Championship</a> tournaments. He scored his first international hat-trick at the <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2018 FIFA World Cup">2018 FIFA World Cup</a> against <a href="/wiki/Panama_national_football_team" title="Panama national football team">Panama</a>, where he would win the tournament's Golden Boot. During the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2020" title="UEFA Euro 2020">UEFA Euro 2020</a> main tournament, he scored 4 goals in the knockout stage as he <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captained</a> the side to their first international final since 1966. During the <a href="/wiki/2022_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2022 FIFA World Cup">2022 FIFA World Cup</a>, he equalled <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Rooney" title="Wayne Rooney">Wayne Rooney</a>'s international record in the quarter-final against <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a>, before surpassing it in 2023 in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2024_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2024 qualifying">UEFA Euro 2024 qualifying</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a>. <h2 id="mp-tfp-h2" class="mp-h2"><span id="Today.27s_featured_picture"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Today's_featured_picture">Today's featured picture</span></h2>
<div id="mp-tfp">

<td><span typeof="mw:File"><a href="/wiki/File:Sea_Girt_Lighthouse_October_2020.jpg" class="mw-file-description" title="Sea Girt Light"><img alt="Sea Girt Light" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Sea_Girt_Lighthouse_October_2020.jpg/550px-Sea_Girt_Lighthouse_October_2020.jpg" decoding="async" width="550" height="413" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Sea_Girt_Lighthouse_October_2020.jpg/825px-Sea_Girt_Lighthouse_October_2020.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Sea_Girt_Lighthouse_October_2020.jpg/1100px-Sea_Girt_Lighthouse_October_2020.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5000" data-file-height="3750" /></a></span>

<td style="padding:0 0.9em; text-align:left;">
<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Sea_Girt_Light" title="Sea Girt Light">Sea Girt Light</a></b> is a <a href="/wiki/Lighthouse" title="Lighthouse">lighthouse</a> marking the inlet leading to the <a href="/wiki/Wreck_Pond" title="Wreck Pond">Wreck Pond</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sea_Girt,_New_Jersey" title="Sea Girt, New Jersey">Sea Girt</a>, New Jersey, United States. It is located on a stretch of the New Jersey shore between the <a href="/wiki/Barnegat_Lighthouse" title="Barnegat Lighthouse">Barnegat</a> and <a href="/wiki/Navesink_Twin_Lights" title="Navesink Twin Lights">Navesink</a> lighthouses. Its site was chosen after a previous proposal for a lighthouse at <a href="/wiki/Manasquan_Inlet" title="Manasquan Inlet">Manasquan Inlet</a> was found infeasible, and it was first lit in December 1896. Protections against sand erosion were added in 1900 and the 1920s, and in 1921, Sea Girt Light was equipped with a radio beacon for use in fog, the first such installation on a shore-based light in the US. At the outset of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the light was deactivated and the lens removed; the house was remodeled to serve as a dormitory for a Coast Guard observation post. At the end of the war, an aerobeacon was mounted atop the tower, with the original lighthouse being decommissioned. In 1955, a new beacon was erected away from the building on a steel tower on the lawn. The building was sold to the borough of Sea Girt shortly after. It was used for the town library and for meeting space for many years, while the beacon itself remained operational until 1977. The building was taken over by the Sea Girt Lighthouse Citizens Committee in 1980 and was subsequently restored. It now serves as a museum, offering tours.
</p>
